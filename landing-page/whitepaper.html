THE COOP Revolution in 2 Minutes

A network of cooperatives, connected by shared infrastructure, shared values, and a shared brand. Each cooperative is independent and sovereign, but all benefit from being part of something bigger.

We're merging the best of two worlds: the democratic governance of cooperatives (180 years of history) with the programmable transparency of DAOs (decentralized autonomous organizations).

The result: organizations where those who work, build, and participate are the owners. Where money doesn't buy power. Where everything is transparent. Where collaboration isn't a slogan on a slide, but the very structure of how things work.

What makes us different

  • Shared ownership: No external shareholders. Those who participate, own.
  • Digital-first: Built for the internet age, accessible from anywhere.
  • Open source: Our code, templates, and knowledge are public goods.
  • DAO-powered: Transparent governance and programmable rules.
  • Born global: No borders, no limitations.

How It Works

A Network, Not a Company

The network is a DAO — a decentralized autonomous organization. It has no owner, no headquarters, no CEO. It exists to guard the purpose and culture of the movement. Nothing more.

The Empty Center

Traditional organizations have a center: a board, a CEO, a headquarters that makes decisions. Our network has an empty center.

The network doesn't govern, doesn't operate, doesn't make decisions. It defined the purpose and principles at the beginning. The rest is up to those who do.

Power is distributed among the cooperatives. Each one is sovereign. The network is the shared space where they coexist.

What the Network Offers

The network is a commons — a shared resource that belongs to everyone and no one. What it offers:

  • Purpose: The reason we exist, the future we want to build.
  • Culture: The principles and values that guide how we do things.
  • Identity: The shared brand and what it represents.
  • Infrastructure: The tools, templates, and technology built by Coop Protocol.
  • Network: Connection between cooperatives and members.

DAO Structure

The network is organized as a DAO with an "empty center". In practice, this means:

  • No central entity making decisions for others
  • No operations or costs at the network level
  • Principles defined at the beginning, not governance ongoing
  • Cooperatives are the ones who act, decide, and operate

Coop-Franchising

A new concept. Traditional franchising has an owner who receives royalties and sets rules. Coop-franchising is different:

  • The brand belongs to the network (commons), not a company
  • Who uses the brand becomes part of its governance
  • Rules decided collectively by franchisees
  • Financial contribution goes to commons, not an owner
  • Quality guaranteed by collective reputation

A Token, Not a Currency

The COOP token is not money. It's identity, history, and signaling. A way to exist in the network and coordinate with others.

What the COOP Token Is

A single token that carries who you are, what you contributed, and what you signal to the network.

  • Soulbound (non-transferable) — represents you, cannot be sold
  • Cumulative — grows with your contributions over time
  • Public — total transparency of history
  • Experimental — starts simple, evolves organically

What it Does (Signaling and Coordination)

  • Signal interest/support for initiatives
  • Coordinate collective attention ("where is the network's energy?")
  • Mobilization mechanism — navigate emergence
  • Express preferences without governing

What it's NOT

  • Doesn't give exclusive access to anything
  • Not voting power in the DAO (empty center, no decisions)
  • Has no direct monetary value
  • Not a traditional governance token

Cooperative Shares

Separate from the COOP token, each cooperative has its own shares:

  • Non-transferable: Cannot be sold or traded
  • Economic participation: Right to surplus/dividends
  • Exit: Leave the coop, get your value back, share is burned

The Experiment

The financial/monetary part of the token is secondary — an experiment. People can make voluntary contributions and we observe how value flows. It's not the engine, it's a scientific curiosity to see what emerges.

Money That Flows

How value circulates in the network. No extraction, no shareholders taking profits. Money flows to those who create value.

Flow 1: Within Each Coop

Each cooperative generates revenue from its business and decides internally:

  • How much goes to operational costs
  • How much goes to reserve/common fund
  • How much is distributed to members

Flow 2: Between Cooperatives

Cooperatives transact directly with each other. Coop Edu hires Coop Cafe for an event space. Coop Protocol provides infrastructure for Coop Bank. Direct payments, no intermediary, no network fee.

Flow 3: Coops to Protocol

Those who use the cloud infrastructure pay a subscription to Protocol. Those who use open source pay nothing. Simple pricing, accessible.

Flow 4: Voluntary Contributions

Anyone can contribute voluntarily to the network treasury. It's an experiment: we inject real value and observe where it flows, what emerges.

Flow 5: Franchises to Collective Brand

Those who use a collective brand (like Coop Cafe) contribute to a common fund managed by all franchisees. This fund is used for collective marketing, developing new models, supporting new franchises.

Dinero Que Circula

Cómo circula el valor en la red. Sin extracción, sin accionistas llevándose ganancias. El dinero fluye hacia quienes crean valor.

Flujo 1: Dentro de Cada Coop

Cada cooperativa genera ingresos de su negocio y decide internamente:

  • Cuánto va a costos operacionales
  • Cuánto va a reserva/fondo común
  • Cuánto se distribuye a los miembros

Flujo 2: Entre Cooperativas

Las cooperativas hacen transacciones directamente entre sí. Coop Edu contrata Coop Cafe para un espacio de evento. Coop Protocol provee infraestructura para Coop Bank. Pagos directos, sin intermediario, sin comisión de red.

Deep Dive

Governance in Detail

The governance tools available for cooperatives in the network.

Legal Base: 1 Person 1 Vote

To be a legal cooperative, the minimum is one person, one vote in fundamental decisions. This is non-negotiable — it's what makes us a cooperative.

But beyond the legal minimum, everything is experimental. Each cooperative can adopt different tools from the menu.

The Menu of Tools

Protocol offers a menu of governance tools. Each coop chooses what makes sense for it:

Voting and Decision

  • Quadratic voting: Express intensity of preference
  • Conviction voting: Weight increases with time of commitment
  • Consent (sociocracy): Passes if no one has reasoned objection
  • Liquid delegation: Delegate your vote by topic to specialists
  • Lazy consensus: Passes automatically if no one objects in X days

Protection and Security

  • Rage quit: Leave with your share if you disagree
  • Minority veto: X% can block a decision
  • Minimum quorum: Decision only valid with X% participation
  • Supermajority: Need 66% or 75% to pass

Ready Templates

Don't want to choose one by one? Protocol offers complete templates:

  • Minimum Legal: 1p1v, annual assembly, elected board
  • Direct Democracy: All decisions go to everyone's vote
  • Delegative: Liquid delegation by topics
  • Sociocratic: Circles + consent
  • Experimental: Conviction voting + quadratic

Technical Infrastructure

What Coop Protocol builds to power the network.

The 6 Layers

1. Identity Layer

Digital wallet, COOP token, universal profile, local profiles per coop, badges and credentials.

2. Cooperative Layer

Coop registry, tokenized shares (non-transferable), member registry, modular governance, treasury, legal templates.

3. Network Layer (DAO)

Network registry, immutable constitution, collective signaling, activity feed, directory.

4. Value/Economy Layer

Inter-coop payments, voluntary contributions, distribution, financial history.

5. Knowledge Layer

Documentation, replicable templates, knowledge base, support.

6. Base Infrastructure

Blockchain (probably L2 for low costs), decentralized storage, open APIs, self-hosted option, cloud option.
