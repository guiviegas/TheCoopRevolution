## Writing Guidelines

### Voice
- First person plural: "We believe...", "We're building..."
- Direct address: "You can...", "Join us..."
- Conversational but not casual

### Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Clear but not dumbed-down
- Serious but not boring
- Visionary but grounded

### Structure
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bullet points for lists
- Bold for key terms
- Tables for comparisons

### What to Avoid
- Jargon without explanation
- Buzzwords without substance
- Overpromising
- Being preachy
- Emojis

## Translation Notes

### Portuguese (PT-BR)
- Use "você" (not "tu")
- "Não" always with accent
- "São", "decisões", "organização" etc.
- 180 anos (not años)

### Spanish (ES)
- Use "tú" for informal
- Question marks at start: ¿...?
- "Cómo", "Qué", "Dónde" with accents
- Different accent pattern than Portuguese

### Keeping in Sync
- Edit all three languages together
- Test all three after changes
- Section IDs are ASCII (no accents)
